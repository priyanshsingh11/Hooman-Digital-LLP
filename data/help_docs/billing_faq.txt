Lumen Billing FAQ

Q: When will I be charged?
Lumen subscriptions renew automatically at the beginning of each billing cycle. Monthly plans renew every 30 days, while annual plans renew once every 12 months unless cancelled beforehand.

Q: Can I switch between plans?
Yes. Customers may upgrade or downgrade plans at any time from the Billing section in Workspace Settings. Upgrades take effect immediately with prorated charges. Downgrades apply at the next renewal cycle.

Q: Why was my card charged after cancellation?
In most cases, this happens when cancellation occurs after the renewal invoice has already been generated. Cancelling a subscription disables future renewals but does not automatically reverse completed charges.

Q: What payment methods are supported?
Lumen accepts major credit cards, ACH transfers for eligible regions, and invoice-based payments for Enterprise accounts.

Q: What happens if my payment fails?
Accounts with failed payments enter a 7-day grace period. During this time, most features remain accessible. After the grace period, accounts may move to past_due status and lose access to premium functionality.

Q: Can I get invoices with tax information?
Yes. Customers may update billing profiles to include VAT IDs, GST numbers, or company registration details. Historical invoices can be regenerated in some regions.

Q: Why do seat counts differ from invoices?
Seat counts update dynamically based on active workspace members. Billing changes may take several minutes to synchronize across systems.

Q: Do you provide refunds?
Refund eligibility depends on plan type, billing date, and account usage. Please review the Lumen Refund Policy for detailed conditions.

Q: Can Enterprise customers use purchase orders?
Yes. Enterprise plans may support purchase orders, annual invoicing, and custom payment terms subject to contract approval.

Q: Where can I view billing history?
Billing history, invoices, receipts, and payment methods are available under Workspace Settings > Billing.
