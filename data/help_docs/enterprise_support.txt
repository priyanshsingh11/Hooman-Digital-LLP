Lumen Enterprise Support Guide

Enterprise customers receive access to advanced support services designed for large organizations and mission-critical workflows.

Enterprise support benefits may include:
- Priority ticket routing
- Faster response time targets
- Dedicated Customer Success Manager
- Technical onboarding assistance
- Architecture consultation
- Security review support
- API migration guidance

Support availability depends on the customer's contract tier and region.

Severity definitions:
Severity 1:
Critical production outage affecting core business operations. Examples include widespread API failures, inaccessible workspaces, or major security incidents.

Severity 2:
Major functionality degraded but workarounds exist.

Severity 3:
General technical questions, configuration guidance, or non-blocking bugs.

Emergency escalation:
Enterprise customers may request emergency escalation through the Priority Support portal. Security-related incidents should include timestamps, affected users, and reproduction details where possible.

Recommended information for technical tickets:
- Workspace ID
- Error messages
- API request IDs
- Browser or app version
- Screenshots or logs
- Time issue first occurred

Enterprise customers using SSO or SCIM should maintain internal identity administrators for first-level troubleshooting.

Support limitations:
Lumen Support does not provide:
- Custom application development
- Debugging third-party customer codebases
- Recovery of permanently deleted data after retention expiration
- Direct management of customer cloud infrastructure

Response targets:
- Severity 1: Initial response within 1 hour
- Severity 2: Initial response within 4 hours
- Severity 3: Initial response within 1 business day

Repeated abuse toward support staff may result in temporary communication restrictions regardless of support tier.

Some Enterprise features require separate enablement by Lumen operations teams before they become visible in workspace settings.
